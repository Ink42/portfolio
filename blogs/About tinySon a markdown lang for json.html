There’s always been something special about having a personal website — a quiet little corner of the internet to call your own. Over time, that curiosity turned into tinkering with home servers and self-hosting, just for the fun of it.

But somewhere along the way, it’s easy to get swept up in the wave of shiny new frameworks — like my old website. One moment it’s simple HTML; the next, you're knee-deep in a build pipeline, wondering how your “basic blog” started needing a PhD in Webpack.

Eventually, it just made sense to pause and simplify. Everyone has their own taste, their own rhythm — and this setup just feels right: clean, low-maintenance, and easy to build on when inspiration strikes.

It’s a quiet little plot in the vast digital landscape — a stable orbit in a galaxy full of wild experiments and blinking UIs. Not the flashiest orbit in the galaxy, but it’s comfy. And hey — it’s home.
